Unicorns

Creatures of myth. Symbols of magic. Absolutely real in our hearts.

📜

Ancient Origins

Unicorns appear in ancient Greek writings not as myth, but as natural history — described by Ctesias around 400 BC as wild donkeys with a single horn. The legend has persisted for over 2,400 years.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿

Scotland's National Animal

Scotland adopted the unicorn as its national animal in the 12th century. In Celtic mythology, the unicorn represents purity, power, and independence — everything Scotland wanted to project to the world.

✨

Magical Powers

Legends say a unicorn's horn — called an alicorn — could neutralize poison and purify water. Medieval kings paid fortunes for what they believed were real unicorn horns (usually narwhal tusks).

🌈

The Rainbow Connection

The association with rainbows comes from their role as creatures of pure light. In many traditions, seeing a unicorn near a rainbow was the highest omen of good fortune a person could receive.

🦋

Only the Pure May Approach

Medieval folklore held that unicorns could only be tamed by someone pure of heart. They were used as symbols of Christ in Christian iconography and appeared frequently in illuminated manuscripts.

💎

Modern Legacy

Today, "unicorn" describes any startup valued over $1 billion — because both are rare, magical, and slightly unbelievable. There are currently over 1,200 unicorn companies worldwide.
